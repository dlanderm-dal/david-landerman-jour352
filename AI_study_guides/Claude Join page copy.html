üîß Different Join Types

1. LEFT JOIN Keeps all rows from LEFT table

employees %>% left_join(departments, by = join_by(dept_id))
emp_id name dept_id dept_name budget
1 Alice 10 Sales 100000
2 Bob 20 Marketing 80000
3 Charlie 10 Sales 100000
4 Diana 30 NA NA
5 Eve NA NA NA
‚úÖ ALL employees are kept (all 5 rows from the left table)
‚ùå HR department is NOT included (it wasn't in the left table)
Diana and Eve get NA values for department info because their dept_ids don't match any department

2. RIGHT JOIN Keeps all rows from RIGHT table

employees %>% right_join(departments, by = join_by(dept_id))
emp_id name dept_id dept_name budget
1 Alice 10 Sales 100000
3 Charlie 10 Sales 100000
2 Bob 20 Marketing 80000
NA NA 40 HR 50000
‚úÖ ALL departments are kept (all 3 rows from the right table)
‚ùå Diana and Eve are NOT included (their dept_ids don't match any department)
HR department gets NA values for employee info because no employees belong to it

3. INNER JOIN Keeps ONLY matching rows

employees %>% inner_join(departments, by = join_by(dept_id))
emp_id name dept_id dept_name budget
1 Alice 10 Sales 100000
2 Bob 20 Marketing 80000
3 Charlie 10 Sales 100000
‚úÖ ONLY employees with valid departments (Alice, Bob, Charlie)
‚ùå Diana excluded (dept_id 30 doesn't exist)
‚ùå Eve excluded (has NA dept_id)
‚ùå HR excluded (no employees in that department)
This is the most restrictive join - only perfect matches!

4. FULL JOIN Keeps ALL rows from BOTH tables

employees %>% full_join(departments, by = join_by(dept_id))
emp_id name dept_id dept_name budget
1 Alice 10 Sales 100000
2 Bob 20 Marketing 80000
3 Charlie 10 Sales 100000
4 Diana 30 NA NA
5 Eve NA NA NA
NA NA 40 HR 50000
‚úÖ ALL employees kept (including Diana and Eve)
‚úÖ ALL departments kept (including HR)
This is the most inclusive join - nothing is lost! NAs fill in where there's no match.

üéØ Using join_by() - Modern Join Syntax

Example 1: Joining on Different Column Names

orders
order_id cust_code amount
1001 CUST_A 500
1002 CUST_B 750
1003 CUST_C 300
customers
customer_id name city
CUST_A Alice Corp NYC
CUST_B Bob Inc LA
CUST_D Dave LLC Chicago
orders %>% left_join(customers, by = join_by(cust_code == customer_id))
order_id cust_code amount name city
1001 CUST_A 500 Alice Corp NYC
1002 CUST_B 750 Bob Inc LA
1003 CUST_C 300 NA NA
Note: With join_by(), use == to match columns with different names. The result keeps the LEFT table's column name (cust_code)!

Wow! This shows all employees who were hired BEFORE each project started. Notice how Alice appears 3 times because she was hired before all 3 projects!
